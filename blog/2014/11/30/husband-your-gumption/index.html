Husband Your Gumption

Nov 30th, 2014 1:05 am

Continuing yesterday’s treatment of gumption and character, it’s time to explicitly highlight an important caveat: Gumption can be depleted! And here too is a chance to connect with Bro Kaizen’s favorite soapbox topic, Deliberate Practice.

Deliberate Practice has 3 inputs:

  1. Time
  2. Knowledge
  3. Gumption

Time management has been abundantly covered by greater powers than Bro Kaizen. Ingredient #2 has been treated elsewhere, on and off this blog. Let’s focus on ingredient #3, “Gumption.”

Therefore you must husband your gumption reserves, protect them from depletion on stupid things, and instead expend them solely on deliberate practice.

Ultimately this is a post about building a system where the desireable outcomes happen by default. Design a trip to the gym into your day by signing up for one that is between your house and job. Put a pullup bar in your bathroom door so you end up doing a few reps on your way to the shower. Pay your buddy to hold your wallet and phone until you complete your 1 hour of Russian language practice.
